The Definition of Awesomeness

The awesomeness that is Josh Antonson cannot be described in a mere 70 words on that joke of a website you call Survivor casting. It is an epic tale of awesomeness that deserves to be told on a website of equal awesomeness.

P.S. You're welcome in advance.

  • 12/03/1992

    On this fateful day, Joshua Antonson entered the world with a full head of hair, a chiseled jawline, and unmatched intelligence. This would set the tone for the rest of his life and put him on the fast track to awesomeness.

  • XC Glory

    After quitting his middle school soccer team, Josh discovers that he can run circles around everyone in his local area. Over the next 4 years, he would go on to become a 2 time state champion, 2 time national qualifier, and captain for his XC team at Neuqua Valley High School.

  • Carnegie Mellon University

    Josh entered Carnegie Mellon University with dreams of being a Mechanical Engineer. After spending 3 months learning about refrigerators and other boring shit, he decided that his awesomeness could be better put to use as an Electrical & Computer Engineer. He would go on to design and build a smart pacing system for athletes, a virtual reality stationary bike, and a web service for postpartum care and scheduling for UPMC all before the ripe age of 21.

  • Palau

    For his first travel experience abroad, Josh did what most people do and decided to go to an English speaking country. Unlike most other people, the country he chose was the small Micronesian nation of Palau.

    In just 3 months, he managed to publically insult the Queen of Palau, spend $2,500 on food, learn to wakeboard by shredding through a national heritage site, and single handedly save a financial organization from shutting down. They are eternally grateful for the awesomeness that is the work of Josh Antonson.

    Fun fact: Palau is the most obese country in the world. Fun fact: Joshua Antonson is the least obese person in the world.

  • Running through Mono

    Halfway through his last XC season at Carnegie Mellon University, doctors told Josh that he had mononucleosis. They said that he wasn't allowed to do any physical activity for 4 weeks and that since the National qualifying meet was only 5 weeks away, his season was over.

    5 weeks later, Josh led his team to qualify for nationals at the Regional XC meet off of sheer willpower, talent, and awesomeness. 1 week after that, he ran the fastest 8k of his life at the National XC Meet to help his team place 18th.

  • TechChange

    Wanting to make a real difference in the world, Josh turned down the high salary life of Silicon Valley and decided to take his talents to a small nonprofit called TechChange in Washington D.C., where he leads the development of an online education platform that has been used to educate students in over 200 countries around the world.

  • Mexico City

    Josh's love of tacos, cervezas, and his girlfriend (in that order) brought him to Mexico City, where he currently resides. Trump's great wall could not stop Josh's awesomeness from crossing the border, much to the dismay of our tiny handed leader.
