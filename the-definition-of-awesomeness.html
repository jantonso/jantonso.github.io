The Definition of Awesomeness

The awesomeness that is Joshua Antonson cannot be described in a mere 70 words on that joke of a website you call Survivor casting. It is an epic tale of awesomeness that deserves to be told on a website of equal awesomeness.

  • 12/03/1992

    On this fateful day, Joshua Antonson was born. You're welcome.

  • Math Genius

    At an early age, teachers discovered that Josh was smarter than the rest of his peers and put him on the fast track.

    While the plebeians were studying Geometry, he was studying 3D Calculus through university.

  • XC Glory

    After quitting his middle school soccer team, Josh discovered that he could run circles around everyone in his local area.

    Over the next 4 years, he would go on to become a 2 time state champion, 2 time national qualifier, and captain for his highschool XC team.

  • Carnegie Mellon University

    Josh entered Carnegie Mellon University with dreams of being a Mechanical Engineer.

    After spending 3 months learning about refrigerators and other boring shit, he decided that his awesomeness could be better put to use as an Electrical & Computer Engineer.

  • Palau

    For his first trip abroad, Josh did what most do and decided to go to an English speaking country.

    Unlike the other peasants, the country he chose was the small Micronesian nation of Palau.

  • In just 3 months, he managed to publically insult the Queen of Palau, spend his entire paycheck on food, learn to wakeboard through a national heritage site, and single handedly save the financial branch of the Palauan government from collapse.

  • Running through Mono

    The doctors diagnosed Josh with Mono and told him that his final XC season was over.

    5 weeks later, Josh led his team to qualify for nationals at the Regional XC meet off of sheer willpower, talent, and awesomeness.

  • TechChange

    Wanting to make a real difference in the world, Josh turned down the safe, high salary bubble of Silicon Valley and decided to take his talents to a small nonprofit called TechChange in Washington D.C. where he currently leads the development of an online education platform.

  • Mexico City

    Josh's love of tacos, cervezas, and his girlfriend (in that order) brought him to Mexico City, where he currently resides.
